# Christoindustry
*An unambiguously good engine of human flourishing. An industry whose profits do not come at the expense of human wellbeing, fleshly desires, or spiritual mediocrity.*

> The flood era is funded, in large part, by industries that profit from human degradation. Pornography, addictive feeds, predatory lending, exploitation logistics, attention-mining entertainment. A Christofuturist village cannot be anchored on those engines. It has to form around an industry whose growth is also human flourishing, whose supply chain is clean enough to disciple inside, and whose revenue is spiritual fruit measured first and money measured second. That kind of industry has a name now. It is a Christoindustry.

---

## The four tests

A Christoindustry has to pass all four tests, not three.

1. **Does it serve human flourishing or exploit human weakness?** The industry's core product makes people more human, more free, more formed, more loved. It does not extract value from loneliness, addiction, lust, vanity, or fear.
2. **Does its supply chain align with kingdom values or compromise them?** The vendors, the manufacturing partners, the platforms, and the distribution channels are either kingdom-aligned or substitutable with kingdom-aligned alternatives. A pure product riding on a compromised supply chain is a pure product about to be captured.
3. **Is success measured spiritually before financially?** Revenue is a vital sign, not the scoreboard. The scoreboard is testimonies, character formation, sub-creation, fruit. A Christoindustry whose only metric is ARR has already drifted.
4. **Does it produce visible fruit beyond revenue?** A Christoindustry leaves a trail of changed lives, formed children, healed marriages, and works of art that point at God. If the only thing the industry produces is profit, it is not yet a Christoindustry; it is a Christian-owned business.

## The named Christoindustries

The list, drawn from the Refound America manifesto and from the empirical Christian-anchored examples already running:

- **Kingdom housing.** Land development, homebuilding, and community design oriented around formation rather than commodity yield.
- **Gospel music.** Recording, publishing, songwriter formation, and worship-music infrastructure that own their own masters and rhythms.
- **Immersive faith experiences.** Themed-entertainment-grade faith experiences, retreats, pilgrimage infrastructure, and the artisan guilds that build them.
- **Healer training.** Physicians, therapists, midwives, and embodied-learning programs that form the next generation of Christian healers.
- **Discipleship infrastructure.** Curriculum, content, software, and physical formation centers that scale the catechesis stack across villages and households.
- **Christofuturist education.** K-12 classical schools, formation academies, and apprenticeship pipelines for trades and arts. (See [classical-school-as-village-anchor](../playbooks/classical-school-as-village-anchor).)
- **Christian university anchor.** A faithful Catholic, Protestant, or Orthodox university operating as the intellectual and credentialing engine of a village. (See [christian-university-as-village-anchor](../playbooks/christian-university-as-village-anchor).)
- **Frontier-science Christoindustries.** Quantum, bio, fusion, AI alignment, and adjacent verticals where Christians can hold the stack from research through deployment without compromising the worldview.

The list grows as Christoindustrialists discover new ones. There is no closed taxonomy.

## The anchoring function

A Christoindustry is not a side-business that the village happens to have. It is the gravitational center the village forms around. The industry generates the revenue that funds the land, the [Christocompound](./christocompound), the schools, the housing, and the civic infrastructure. The industry magnetizes the talent that wants to live near its center. The industry produces the testimonies that make the village legible to outsiders looking for a way out of the flood.

If the village has a "main industry" and a list of separate ministries on the side, the main industry has not yet become a Christoindustry. It is still secular work with religious accessories. The arc finishes when the industry itself is doing the discipling.

## Industry sovereignty

A real Christoindustry holds enough of its own stack to resist capture or compromise. Sovereignty means: the supply chain, the IP, the capital base, and the talent pipeline are inside the kingdom-aligned network or are credibly substitutable from inside it. A music Christoindustry that owns the masters and the publishing stack is sovereign. One that licenses everything from a hostile distributor is one corporate-policy change away from extinction. A healer-training Christoindustry that owns its credentialing body is sovereign. One that depends on accreditation from an actively hostile guild is borrowed time.

The sufficient-sovereignty bar is not vertical-integration purism. It is enough sovereignty to refuse a coerced compromise without losing the business. Christoindustrialists should be able to name, on demand, the three points in their stack where loss of sovereignty would force the worst trade.

## Christoindustry vs Christian-owned business

The Christian-owned coffee shop is not necessarily a Christoindustry. The Christian-owned LLC selling commodity products into a commodity supply chain is not necessarily a Christoindustry. The test is structural. Does this entire industry, as a whole, serve flourishing? Does it pass the four tests as an industry, not just as a single business inside it?

Most Christian-owned businesses are good. Most Christian-owned businesses are not, on their own, Christoindustries. The Christoindustry is the industry-level structure inside which a Christian-owned business can grow without becoming an exception to its sector.

## Common failure modes

- **Industry drift.** Commercial success dilutes the founding mission. The supply chain widens, the customer base widens, and one quarter at a time the four tests stop being run. See [cultural-drift-risk](./cultural-drift-risk).
- **Supplier-cluster effect that pulls non-aligned partners in.** The cluster the industry generates includes vendors and ecosystem players whose values do not match. If the Christoindustrialist does not actively curate the cluster, the cluster recurates the industry. See [supplier-cluster-effect](./supplier-cluster-effect).
- **Sovereignty erosion by acquisition.** A Christoindustrialist sells one stack layer at a time for liquidity. Five years later the industry depends on partners who can dictate its terms.
