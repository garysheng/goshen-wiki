# Semi-permeable boundaries
*The Goshen pattern: thick spiritual core at the center (university, parish, school with mission-shaped admissions), legally permeable shell on the outside (open-market housing, federally protected non-discrimination). Catholic identity at the center, open town legally.*

> A Christoindustrialist village cannot be a closed religious enclave under US law. It also cannot be a generic master-planned community. The only available shape is semi-permeable: a thick spiritual core at the center, a legally open shell on the outside. Done well, this is the strongest available pattern for a Christofuturist village in the United States. Done poorly, it produces both the "Catholic Jonestown" media attack and the cultural drift that comes from too-thin filtering.

---

## What it is

Semi-permeable boundaries is the operating geometry where:

- **The center is thick.** The anchor institutions (university, parish, classical school, Christocompound) use mission-shaped admissions, theological covenants, doctrinal commitments, and formation-thick rhythms. Joining them is a deliberate act with theological content.
- **The shell is open.** The housing market is legally non-discriminatory. Homes are sold to anyone. The town is publicly described as welcoming all denominations. The shell satisfies the federal housing law and the local political environment.

The mission-shaped center filters for committed residents through self-selection. People who want to live near the church choose to live there. People who do not, do not. The legal shell prevents the project from being attacked as a coercive confessional enclave.

## Why it works

The pattern works for three reasons.

First, **self-selection is a stronger filter than gatekeeping.** People who buy homes near a Catholic university with a parish church at the visual center of town are mostly people who want to live near a Catholic university and parish. The market does the filtering without anyone having to write a discriminatory housing policy.

Second, **legal openness defuses the political attack.** The "Catholic Jonestown" framing that hit Ave Maria in 2006 over the contraceptives controversy is the cost of any ambiguity about whether the town is open or closed. A clear, public, repeated commitment to open housing prevents the framing from sticking. The town can be culturally distinctive while remaining legally normal.

Third, **the mission-thick institutions hold the line on identity.** The town can be demographically mixed and the parish, school, and university can still be formationally serious. The anchors are where the catechesis happens. The town is where the people live who want to be near it.

## Ave Maria as the canonical example

Ave Maria has run this posture since launch. The developer has consistently insisted the town is open to all denominations and that homes are sold without discrimination. Donahue Academy and Ave Maria University both use mission-shaped admissions and run formation rhythms that filter for committed participants:

- Ave Maria University identifies explicitly as faithful to the Magisterium, runs daily Mass, confession, rosary, spiritual direction, and over 30 faith-based clubs.
- Donahue Academy is parish-operated, classical, Catholic, with daily Mass and frequent confession.

The town shell is open. The institutional core is thick. That is the canonical pattern.

## Tradeoffs

The honest costs a Christoindustrialist must price in:

- **[Cultural drift risk](./cultural-drift-risk) increases over time.** As the town becomes legible to the broader real-estate market, the demographic of the shell drifts toward people who want the amenities rather than the mission. By Ave Maria's tenth year the developer himself was already noting this drift. The mission-thick institutions hold, but the surrounding town becomes more demographically generic.
- **Founder ambiguity produces media attacks.** Any public statement from the founder or developer that sounds like the town might restrict residency on religious grounds becomes ammunition. Ave Maria's 2006 contraceptives controversy and the ACLU response is the canonical example. The Christoindustrialist must be disciplined about messaging.
- **The thick institutions do most of the work.** A semi-permeable village without strong anchor institutions has no center to hold the identity. The pattern only works if the [Christian university as village anchor](../playbooks/christian-university-as-village-anchor) and the [classical school as village anchor](../playbooks/classical-school-as-village-anchor) are doing serious formation. Weak anchors plus open shell equals a generic suburb in five years.

## How to apply it

Concrete moves for a Christoindustrialist planning a Christofuturist village:

1. **Build with the explicit assumption of legally open housing.** Do not pretend you can write religious filtering into the deed restrictions. Federal housing law will not allow it and the political cost will swamp the project.
2. **Front-load the mission-thick institutions.** The university, the school, and the parish must be operational before the housing volume gets large enough to dilute the founding demographic. The institutional core has to be the magnet.
3. **Write clear, public, repeated messaging about the open shell.** Pre-empt the "coercive enclave" media attack with explicit, repeated, on-the-record statements about open housing. Do not leave the framing to opponents.
4. **Use voluntary covenants and rituals at the center.** What the legal shell cannot enforce, the institutional rhythms can. Membership in the parish, enrollment in the school, sacramental participation, public liturgical life. The covenants are voluntary and that is what makes them load-bearing.
5. **Plan for drift compensation.** Pair this pattern with the defenses on the [cultural drift risk](./cultural-drift-risk) page: explicit residency covenants, regular catechetical re-grounding, housing-mix discipline.

## The Christofuturist application

The Christoindustrialist building from scratch in the 2020s and 2030s should treat semi-permeable boundaries as the only viable shape under US law and culture. The closed-enclave option is legally and politically dead. The fully open option does not produce a Christofuturist village. The semi-permeable shape is what is left, and Ave Maria proves it can work.

The Christofuturist tweak on Ave Maria is to tighten the institutional core while keeping the legal shell open. More serious mission-shaped admissions. More frequent catechetical re-grounding. More explicit covenants. The shell stays open; the center gets denser. That is how the next generation of Christofuturist villages avoids becoming amenity-rich communities with steeples.
