# Village research prompt
*A copy-pasteable prompt for researching any village or intentional community you find interesting. Use with Claude, GPT, Gemini, or any capable LLM. Output is a structured report keyed to Christofuturist village concepts.*

---

When you encounter a village, intentional community, church-anchored town, historical commune, or any place-based experiment in living together that catches your attention, you do not need to start research from scratch. The Goshen wiki has named concepts (Christoindustrialist, Christocompound, Christoindustry, Christoculture, the Glory of Zion pattern, sufficient sovereignty, the mortgage snare) and the report format below pulls signal aligned with those.

Use it for Christian communities, secular intentional communities (Zuzalu, Praxis, Auroville, Findhorn), historical experiments (Bruderhof, Hutterites, Ephrata Cloister, Catholic Worker farms), and failures (Jonestown, communes that collapsed). The framework adapts. Failures teach as much as successes.

Fill the placeholders, paste, send.

## The prompt

````
You are helping me research [VILLAGE / COMMUNITY NAME] for the Goshen wiki, an operator's playbook for Christofuturist villages. Goshen wiki: https://goshen.wiki

I want to understand this community in enough depth to extract useful patterns, lessons, and failure modes for future Christofuturist village-builders.

KNOWN INFO:
- Name: [name]
- Location: [if known]
- Source URL or how I found it: [if known]
- Anchoring leader(s): [if known]
- Anchoring industry, ministry, or calling: [if known]
- One-line why I find it interesting: [free text]

GIVE ME A STRUCTURED RESEARCH REPORT:

## 1. Snapshot
- Where it is (geography, region, country, urban/rural)
- Who anchors it (founders, current leaders, succession status)
- What anchors it (the industry, ministry, mission, or shared calling that holds it together)
- Founded when, current population or membership

## 2. Origin story
- How it formed
- What problem the founders were solving
- Key inflection moments

## 3. Spiritual / worldview anchor
- Denomination, tradition, or worldview
- Key doctrines or distinctives
- Worship and discipleship rhythms (or secular equivalents)
- Relationship to mainstream Christianity (if Christian) or to mainstream culture

## 4. Land and infrastructure
- Land acquired how (greenfield, revival of dying town, abandoned-property reuse, multi-parcel assembly, urban infill)
- Is there a Christocompound-equivalent central convergence point (worship + food + learning + healthcare + community under one roof or campus)?
- Sovereignty over energy, water, food, sewage, internet?
- Building and zoning approach (own incorporated town, unincorporated land, suburb of existing city, no land ownership at all)

## 5. Economy and capital
- How members earn a living
- Shared treasury, kingdom enterprises, individual businesses, or mixed?
- Original funding source (founder wealth, donations, member buy-in, organic profits)
- Supply-chain sovereignty?
- How housing works (own, lease, communal, what role does the mortgage snare play)

## 6. Education and formation
- Schools (own school, private outside, homeschool co-op, public school, none)
- Apprenticeship or vocational pipelines
- Multi-generational rhythms (kids learning from elders directly)
- Posture toward technology (Luddite, embracing, selective, AI-augmented)

## 7. Governance and membership
- Who decides what (eldership, council, founder-as-final-authority, congregational vote, charter)
- How families join (open enrollment, invitation, vetted application, born-into-it)
- How conflict is handled
- How members can leave (frictionless or costly)
- Boundaries with surrounding world (closed, semi-permeable, fully integrated)

## 8. Community culture / Christoculture
- What it feels like to be there
- Daily and weekly rhythms
- Customs, holidays, signature practices
- Relationship to art, food, music, technology

## 9. Track record and fruit
- Visible outcomes (children grown well, businesses launched, miracles, conversions, art produced)
- Growth pattern over time (linear, exponential, plateau, declining)
- External recognition or critique
- Controversies or scandals

## 10. Failure modes and risks
- Where the community is structurally fragile
- Historical schisms or near-collapses
- Succession risk
- Cultural drift risk (lukewarm-ification)
- Whether the community has failed already, and if so why

## 11. Lessons for a Christofuturist village-builder
- What patterns from this community are reusable in a Christofuturist village?
- What patterns are warning signs?
- Which named Goshen-wiki concepts does this community embody, contradict, or refine? (Christoindustrialist, Christocompound, Christoindustry, Christoculture, the Glory of Zion pattern of heart → relationship → org → infra → territory, the mortgage snare, sufficient sovereignty, the Ark Village framework, the patronage renaissance, divine principle first design)
- What would I copy, what would I avoid, what would I tweak?

## 12. Sources
- Cite specific URLs, books, podcasts, articles, interviews, transcripts you drew from
- Flag where information is sparse, contested, or unverifiable

If you do not have enough information on a section, say so explicitly rather than fabricating. If the community is non-Christian, still apply the framework and adapt the spiritual section honestly. If the community has failed, give equal weight to what went wrong as to what went right.
````

## How to use the output

The LLM's report is raw material, not a wiki page. Take what is useful and:

- **Route the strongest lifts through `/goshen-field-notes`** with the report as pasted text input. The skill will triage the lifts and graduate the page-worthy ones into `concepts/`, `case-studies/`, or `playbooks/`.
- **For a village worth dedicated coverage**, the report becomes the raw material for a `case-studies/<community-slug>.md` entry. The voice rules still apply (hot opener, cooler body, named concepts, cross-links to FaithWalk OS).
- **For lessons that are not village-specific**, route to `concepts/` (a named pattern) or `playbooks/` (a vertical recipe).

The point of the report is to never start research from a blank page again. Every village you encounter has the same dozen questions worth asking, and the same set of Goshen-wiki concepts worth measuring against.
