# Lexicon
*Short A-Z definitions of the named concepts used across the Goshen wiki. Each entry is a sentence-or-two definition with a pointer to where the concept is developed.*

---

## Ark Village
A Spirit-filled village of obedient Imago Dei creators living near each other on purpose. The framework is plural and networked; never one giant total ark. Full concept on [FaithWalk OS](https://faithwalk.garysheng.com/concepts/ark-village) (gated).

## Christocompound
The convergence point inside a Christofuturist village where worship, food, learning, daycare, healthcare, and community physically meet. Often a single multipurpose building or campus.

## Christoculture
The distinct local culture of a specific Christofuturist village. Two villages can share the same Christ-first foundation while developing different worship styles, customs, industries, denominational flavors, and rhythms.

## Christofuturism
Keeping God first in an age of miracle-making machines. The umbrella worldview the Goshen wiki sits inside. Full concept on [FaithWalk OS](https://faithwalk.garysheng.com/concepts/christofuturism) (gated).

## Christoindustrialist
The leader who anchors a Christofuturist village. A leader who has received God's heart for a specific people and place and who anchors a Christoindustry around which a community forms.

## Christoindustry
An unambiguously good engine of human flourishing. An industry whose profits do not come at the expense of human wellbeing, fleshly desires, or spiritual mediocrity. Examples: kingdom housing, gospel music, immersive faith experiences, healer training, discipleship infrastructure.

## Goshen
The biblical archetype for a prosperous, protected, set-apart land God gives His people while they live in a fallen Egypt. Every Christofuturist village is, in spirit, a Goshen. Also the nickname Apostle Delmar Coward Jr. uses for the aspirational village in Liberty, Texas.

## Real Goshen
The specific aspirational Christofuturist village in Liberty County, Texas, anchored on Encounter Church and led by Apostle Delmar Coward Jr. Not operational yet; included as a case study of an in-progress village.
